Oral History

Interviews, memory, and lived experience as historical evidence.

What counts as evidence

Oral histories capture details that rarely appear in official archives: accent, gesture, food smells, family jokes, silences, and the meaning people attach to places.

This page organizes interviews by themes such as migration routes, language shift, work and schooling, foodways, and ritual life.
