Building North America & The Pacific

1840s–1940s: Gold Rush, Railroads, and Exclusion

SCROLL
Overview

Phase II marks the era of mass migration to the "Gold Mountains" of California, Australia, and Canada. It focuses on how Cantonese migrants built the infrastructure of the Pacific world while navigating severe racial exclusion and forming resilient enclaves (Chinatowns).

Key patterns
  • The Coolie Trade and indentured labor
  • Formation of the "Bachelor Society" and transnational families
  • Institutional resilience (Associations) against the Exclusion Acts
